# Profile Settings: account, email, and preferences

Profile Settings help you manage the basic details of your Centuply account.

From here, you can review information such as name, email, language, notifications, preferences, and settings that affect your experience inside the app.

## When to use this guide

Use this guide when you want to:

- review account details,
- change display name,
- check or update email,
- change preferred language,
- configure notifications,
- check security details,
- change app preferences,
- prepare the account before creating or publishing a website.

## Step 1 — Open Profile Settings

Open the Centuply app and open the account menu.

It is usually located in the top-right area, on the avatar, user name, or account menu.

Then select **Profile**, **Settings**, **Account Settings**, or **Profile Settings**.

## Step 2 — Review basic details

Basic details may include information such as:

- name,
- email,
- company name,
- account type,
- profile image,
- account creation date,
- connected websites or projects.

Make sure the details are correct, especially if they are used for billing, invoices, or communication.

## Step 3 — Update display name

The display name may be used inside the app, in collaboration features, or in notifications.

Use a clear name, such as:

- your personal name,
- business name,
- team name,
- account owner name.

Avoid temporary or unclear names if the account is used professionally.

## Step 4 — Check account email

The account email is important because it may be used for:

- login,
- notifications,
- billing emails,
- payment confirmations,
- password reset,
- website updates,
- support communication.

If you change the email, check whether email verification is required.

## Step 5 — Set language

If supported, you can choose the preferred language for the app interface.

Examples:

- Greek,
- English,
- Spanish.

The app language may affect menus, labels, helper messages, and some default options.

It is not necessarily the same as the language of the website you create.

## Step 6 — Check notifications

In notifications, you can configure what you want to receive.

Notification examples:

- form submissions,
- leads,
- booking requests,
- billing updates,
- publish status,
- domain status,
- support replies,
- product updates.

Keep important notifications enabled for your website operations.

## Step 7 — Review app preferences

Depending on available settings, you may review preferences such as:

- default language,
- default website,
- editor preferences,
- preview behavior,
- theme or appearance,
- timezone,
- regional format,
- email preferences.

These settings help Centuply better match the way you work.

## Step 8 — Check security details

If security options are available, check:

- password,
- connected login methods,
- active sessions,
- two-factor authentication, if supported,
- recent login activity,
- recovery email.

Use a strong password and do not share login details.

## Step 9 — Check connected websites

If your account has multiple websites, check which projects are connected to the profile.

This helps when:

- you have multiple draft websites,
- you have published websites,
- you collaborate with others,
- you need to find which site relates to billing or support,
- you want to organize projects.

## Step 10 — Save changes

After each change, click **Save**, **Update**, or the relevant option.

After saving, refresh if needed and check that the changes appear correctly.

## Best practices

- Use a professional email.
- Keep billing and account details updated.
- Do not use a shared password.
- Keep notifications for leads and billing enabled.
- Do not change email unless you can access the new inbox.
- Keep language and timezone correct.
- Regularly check whether account details are correct.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I change my email?

If supported by your account, yes. Email verification may be required.

### Does profile language change the website language too?

Not necessarily. App language and website language may be different.

### Why am I not receiving notifications?

Check notification settings, spam folder, and whether the account email is correct.

### Should I use a professional email?

For business websites, it is better to use an email that is checked often and belongs to the business.

### What should I do if I cannot log in?

Use password reset or contact support, but never send full login credentials.
